A Timeline of Octavia E. Butler's Life and Immortality Through Her Writing:

  • June 22, 1947 Octavia Estelle Butler is born in Pasadena, California
  • Late 1950s her mother buys her a typewriter and she begins writing science fiction, persevering even after an aunt kindly tells her, then 13, that "Honey...Negroes can't be writers."
  • Mid 1960s enters the Pasadena Community College short-story contest and wins both the top prize & $15
  • 1971 sells two stories. One, "Crossover," becomes her first published story when it prints in an anthology that same year
  • 1978 finally earns enough money through writing to stop working temp jobs
  • 1984 "Speech Sounds" wins the Hugo Award for Best Short Story & "Bloodchild" wins the Nebula Award for Best Novelette
  • 1985 "Bloodchild" wins the Science Fiction Chronicle Award, the Locus Award, and the Hugo Award for Best Novelette
  • 1995 Octavia E. Butler wins the MacArthur Foundation "Genius" Grant, becoming the first science fiction writer to ever be awarded the $295,000 prize
  • 1999 Parable of the Talents wins the Nebula Award for Best Novel
  • 2000 wins the PEN American Center Lifetime Achievement Award in Writing
  • 2005 inducted into Chicago State University's International Black Writers Hall of Fame
  • February 24th, 2006 Octavia Estelle Butler passed away, age 58
  • 2010 posthumously inducted into the Science Fiction Hall of Fame
  • 2012 posthumously awarded the Solstice Award by the Science Fiction & Fantasy Writers of America
  • 2017 Parable of the Sower, about a near-future Los Angeles where American society has collapsed, is adapted into an opera
  • 2018 Kindred, where the lead character Dana, a young black woman, is pulled back and forth between current day and an 1800s plantation, is adapted into a Graphic Novel & awarded the Eisner Award for Best Adaptation from Another Medium
  • 2019 Amazon Prime Video begins developing Wild Seed, about two African immortals and their long lives
  • September 2020 dystopia Parable of the Sower (originally published in 1993) appears on the New York Times bestseller list for the first time
  • 2021 NASA names the landing site of the Perseverance rover "Octavia E. Butler Landing"
  • 2021 FX begins production on time-travel tale Kindred
  • 2021 A24 Studios wins rights to adapt the Parable of the Sower for the silver screen
  • 2021 HBO pilot script adaptation of Fledging, a science fiction vampire novel, is ordered
